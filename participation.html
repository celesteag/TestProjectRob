Steps to get started

1

Step 1 (August) – Find teams

Find a teacher with an IT or robotic course at your school, willing to participate. He has then to form up to 6 teams (2–3 students in each team).

2

Step 2 (August) – Organize LEGO robots

Organize LEGO Mindstorms sets. Borrow them from other schools or buy them at eBay. The following sets are allowed:

  • Best!: LEGO Mindstorms EV3 Education Core Set 45544 (eBay)
  • LEGO® MINDSTORMS® EV3 31313 (eBay)
  • LEGO® MINDSTORMS® MINDSTORMS® NXT 2.0 8547-1 (eBay)
  • LEGO® Education SPIKE™ Prime
  • Arduino Robotic Kit
3

Step 3 (September) – Registration

Fill in your registration here: Registration
More info: info@roboeurope.com

4

Step 4 (September) – Prepare your students

They will only know about the task on the day of the combat. Suggestions:

  • Build a robot and race with a remote (see pictures in gallery)
  • Use mobile apps for remote control
  • Follow a black line with colour sensor
  • Follower race with decision-making (turn left/right)
  • PID regulation testing
  • Navigation maze
  • Play Tic-Tac-Toe
5

Step 5 (October) – Infrastructure

You need a 2×1 m table with a wall, 10 cm high. Construct all the obstacles. Details: here

6

Step 6 (January) – Streaming setup

Organize a YouTube stream. Prepare cameras, beamers and lighting. You will receive streaming test scenes weeks before the combat.

7

Step 7 (January) – Printing the layout

You receive rules and simulation. Print the poster and layout. PDF and resources: here

8

Step 8 (February) – Day of the combat

Each school runs its own 10-min combat. All achieved points count toward world ranking. Presentation for combat: here
